1. Batch Registration & NFT Creation

The farmer begins by registering their harvest batch through the AgriChain mobile app. This creates a digital record of the agricultural produce with essential metadata.

Capture Essential Data: Crop type, estimated weight, harvest date, GPS location, and photos
Generate Unique Identifier: System creates a unique batch ID and QR code
Mint Batch NFT: Backend mints a Non-Fungible Token on Hedera Token Service representing the batch
Store Metadata: Batch details stored on IPFS with hash recorded on Hedera Consensus Service

This creates an immutable, tamper-proof record of the batch's origin that can be verified by all subsequent participants in the supply chain.

2. Custody Transfer & Tracking

When the batch changes hands from farmer to transporter, each custody transfer is recorded on the blockchain, creating an immutable chain of custody.

Scan QR Code: Transporter scans the batch QR code using their mobile app
Record Transfer: System records custody transfer with timestamp and GPS location
Submit to Blockchain: Custody event submitted to Hedera Consensus Service as immutable record
Update Status: Batch status updated to "In Transit" with estimated delivery time

Each custody transfer creates a new entry in the batch's provenance timeline, building trust through transparency at every step.

3. Quality Inspection & Verification

At key points in the supply chain, authorized inspectors verify the batch's quality, condition, and compliance with standards.

Quality Assessment: Inspector evaluates weight, quality, temperature, and condition
Record Findings: Inspection results recorded with photos and sensor data
Blockchain Verification: Inspection hash submitted to Hedera for immutable verification
Compliance Check: System verifies batch meets export and regulatory requirements

This creates trusted quality verification that buyers can rely on, reducing disputes and enabling premium pricing for quality produce.

4. Escrow Payment & Fund Release

The final step ensures secure, timely payment to farmers through our automated escrow system powered by Hedera smart contracts.

Buyer Places Funds: Buyer transfers payment to escrow account upon order confirmation
Delivery Confirmation: System detects delivery completion through final custody transfer
Automated Release: Smart contract automatically releases funds to farmer upon delivery confirmation
Transaction Record: Payment transaction recorded on Hedera for complete audit trail

This eliminates payment delays and ensures farmers receive fair compensation promptly, improving cash flow and financial stability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do farmers need smartphones or internet access to use AgriChain?

While we offer a full-featured mobile app for smartphones, we also provide SMS and USSD fallback options for farmers with basic phones or limited internet access. Local extension officers can also assist with batch registration using shared devices.

How much does it cost to use AgriChain?

Transaction costs on Hedera are extremely low - approximately $0.0001 per custody event. For farmers, basic usage is free. Exporters and buyers pay subscription fees based on volume, which are typically offset by savings from reduced disputes, spoilage, and administrative costs.

How does AgriChain ensure data privacy?

We follow a "hash on-chain, data off-chain" approach. Only cryptographic hashes of batch data are stored on the blockchain, while sensitive information is stored in encrypted form on IPFS. Participants control what data is shared and with whom through selective disclosure mechanisms.

Can AgriChain integrate with existing systems?

Yes, we provide APIs and integration tools to connect with existing ERP, accounting, and supply chain management systems. We've designed AgriChain to complement rather than replace existing workflows.

What happens if there's a dispute about quality or delivery?

The immutable audit trail on Hedera provides clear evidence of custody transfers, quality inspections, and condition at each stage. This transparency significantly reduces disputes, and when they do occur, provides verifiable data for resolution.
